Encode a message using the Caesar Cipher

What is the Caesar Cipher?

Attributed to Julius Caesar, the Caesar cipher is one of the oldest known methods of cryptology. It is a substitution cipher, meaning the letters in the cipher are substituted using a shift.

How does it work?

Alice and Bob want to share a private message with each other but they know Eve is listening. To keep their message secret, they move every letter in their message by an agreed shift. If the shift is 3, for example, 'a' becomes 'd', 'b' becomes 'e' and so on.
